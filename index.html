Customizing a Nametag

Custom Nametag Project

Workflow Steps

I downloaded a Nametag 3MF file from Brandeis's Public Workshop, opened it in PrusaSlicer, and put in my name. I changed the font as well!

Many thanks to everyone who contributes to engineering and maker resources on campus!

Getting Ready

I adjusted the settings in the upper-right corner according to the printer and filament used in the project. After clicking "Slice Now" in the bottom right corner, the anatomy of my print showed up. You can see the texture of this upcoming print!

Then I click "Export G-Code" and save the file to a U-drive. Check the print bed and the filament, and plug in the U-drive. It's time for the printer to start working!

Comparison

I printed a nametag the semester before in an English class about medieval studies and maker culture. It was in a different font!

Compared to the font I used at that time, the font I'm using this time was printed more coarsely.
